Praxis concepts

A small reference window for how this site is structured. Scroll to explore more concepts.

Showing 4 at a time (2×2). Scroll inside the window to view more.

Scroll

CTMF

building blocks

Reusable Concepts, Tools, Models, and Frameworks. Each CTMF is linked to evidence of use and a short utility assessment.

FDCT

process loop

Frame → Diverge → Converge → Test. A pragmatic loop to keep work evidence-first and iteration-driven.

Stakeholder map

people

Who is affected, who decides, and what “success” means for each group. Useful for aligning criteria and messaging.

Requirements & metrics

frame

Translate vague complaints into testable criteria and measurable success metrics. Prevents feature creep.

Assumption log

risk

List assumptions, their risk, and how to validate them. Helps plan tests before committing to high effort builds.

Proxy test

test

Fast, low-fidelity tests to validate key claims early. Turns “seems good” into evidence.

Measurement matrix

compare

Compare concepts with numbers (time, steps, reliability, size). Great for transparent convergence.

PUGH chart

converge

Structured comparison vs a reference concept. Useful when you need a defensible selection rationale.

Pairwise comparison

weights

Resolve close calls by comparing criteria one pair at a time. Produces clearer priorities and weights.

Evidence trail

navigation

Claim → Evidence → So-what. Every major claim should be near a figure/table/report link.

Failure modes

engineering

Identify how a system fails before optimizing. Guides safe convergence and reduces fragile designs.

Reflection loop

learning

What worked, what failed, what I would change. Converts project work into reusable personal practice.

Process

A simple structure to keep work rigorous and reusable.

01
Frame

02
Diverge
Generate options, explore alternatives, gather evidence.
03
Converge
Decide with tradeoffs, test, iterate, document rationale.
04
Test
Verify claims; validate with stakeholder feedback when possible.
