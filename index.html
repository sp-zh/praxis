Praxis concepts

A small “legend” so a reader can understand how my pages are structured (and how to navigate the evidence).

CTMF（Concepts, Tools, Models, Frameworks）

portfolio building blocks

A CTMF is something I reuse across projects: a Concept（概念）, Tool（工具）, Model（模型）, or Framework（框架）. Each CTMF on this site is linked to: (1) evidence of use, and (2) a short assessment of utility/fit.

  • Example tools: requirements table, measurement matrix, PUGH chart, pairwise comparison
  • Example frameworks: FDCT loop, stakeholder mapping

FDCT（Frame, Diverge, Converge, Test）

process loop

A practical iteration loop I use to keep work evidence-first: Frame（定义） the problem and success metrics → Diverge（发散） options → Converge（收敛） with explicit tradeoffs → Test（测试） to validate claims.

  • Frame: constraints, stakeholders, measurable success
  • Test: proxy tests, stakeholder feedback, verification tables

Tools（常用工具）

decision + evidence
  • Requirements table（需求表）: turns complaints into testable criteria.
  • Measurement matrix（测量矩阵）: compares concepts with numbers, not vibes.
  • PUGH chart（PUGH 图）: structured comparison vs a reference concept.
  • Pairwise comparison（两两比较）: resolves close calls through debate + judging.
  • Proxy test（代理测试）: fast evidence before high-fidelity builds.

Evidence trail（证据链）

navigation

Every major claim should have a nearby link to supporting evidence (figure/table/report/video). This makes grading fast and makes the work reusable for future projects.

  • Pattern: Claim → Evidence → So-what
  • Rule: if a reader can’t find evidence in 10 seconds, it’s not “documented” yet.
