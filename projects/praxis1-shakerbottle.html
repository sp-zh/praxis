1) Overview

What problem, for whom, and what “success” means.

  • Context: Shaker bottles are used for protein/milkshake mixing, but daily use reveals recurring pain points (mixing, cleaning, leakage, portability, etc.).
  • Stakeholders: primary users (gym / students / commuters), secondary stakeholders (people nearby—noise/odor concerns; manufacturers—cost/assembly).
  • Problem statement（问题陈述）: 【TODO: 1–2 sentences, your version】
  • Success metrics（成功指标）: e.g., leakage resistance, mixing performance (clump reduction), cleaning time, odor retention, ease-of-use, and manufacturability/cost.
Tip: 尽量把 success metrics 写成“可测试”的句子，比如：“10 次摇晃后无明显结块”、“清洗时间 ≤ 30 秒”、“倒置 30 秒无渗漏”等（把阈值改成你实际的）。

4) Requirements & constraints

Turn messy user feedback into checkable requirements.

  • Functional requirements: must mix effectively; must seal reliably; must be easy to clean; must be safe for food contact.
  • Non-functional requirements: low-cost/low-part-count; durable; comfortable ergonomics; minimal odor retention; visually understandable.
  • Constraints: 【TODO: materials available, time, tools, budget, manufacturing assumptions】

8) CTMF mapping + assessment

Show evidence-of-use, and evaluate what worked for you.

  • Frame — Requirements table（需求表） + stakeholder map（利益相关者）: Turned user pain points into testable requirements and success metrics.
    Evidence: link your requirements table / notes (PDF) in Deliverables or Evidence.
    Assessment: prevented scope drift; made “what to test” obvious.
  • Diverge — Morphological chart（形态学表） / SCAMPER（SCAMPER） / Crazy-8s（Crazy-8s）: Generated multiple mechanism + lid concepts before selecting a direction.
    Evidence: sketches/photos of brainstorming outputs.
    Assessment: useful to break fixation on a single “obvious” design.
  • Converge — Decision matrix（决策矩阵） / Pugh matrix（Pugh）: Chose final direction with explicit criteria and tradeoffs.
    Evidence: matrix screenshot/table + final selection reasoning.
    Assessment: made the decision defensible and communicable.
  • Represent — Annotated photos + structured narrative（注释图 + 结构化叙事）: Communicated the design story: claim → evidence → decision.
    Evidence: this page + one-pager.
    Assessment: improved clarity for assessors and teammates.
